The governing rule
Reply detection exits the sequence

The instant a contact replies, they leave the automated flow and a person takes over scheduling. No lead should ever receive an automated "just bumping this" email after they've already answered.

Every send carries the implicit condition: only if no reply has been received.

Two positive off-ramps

Booking via the scheduling link exits the sequence entirely.

Clicking the link but not finishing routes to a short booking-recovery note (Email A) rather than the next nurture email.

Micro-personas
The Generous Mentor

Senior alum (Partner / MD / Principal). Time-poor but emotionally motivated to give back and mentor students. Moved by Trojan identity, legacy, and being thanked rather than sold to.

Targeted byEmail 1's "fellow Trojan" open, and Email 4, which thanks them and leaves the door open with zero guilt.

The Busy Operator

Mid-to-senior alum (VP / Director). Slammed, protective of time, and instinctively reads outreach as a sales trap. Needs specificity, brevity, the explicit "not pitching," and a one-tap path.

Targeted byEmail 1's low-friction framing, Email 2's 15-minute flex, and the scheduling link throughout.

The Curious Peer

Junior alum (Associate / Senior Associate). Recently a student, intellectually engaged, relates to the research itself. Won't move for "help a student," but will move for an interesting idea.

Targeted byEmail 3, which leads with the finding, shrinks the ask to one question, and offers to share results back.

Why this covers everyone

Each persona is reached by a different email, so a single sequence covers the whole recipient spectrum without segmenting the list up front.

Note: if your course requires your own validated micro-personas, swap these in — the structure maps cleanly onto whichever set you're graded against.

Strategy rationale
Escalating delays (0 → 4 → 9 → 16)

Early follow-ups are tight because inbox attention decays fast; later ones widen because someone who hasn't replied by Day 9 needs space, not pressure.

Each email changes its job

Email 1 asks. Email 2 fights for visibility. Email 3 adds value and shrinks the ask. Email 4 releases pressure and banks goodwill.

A sequence that just re-sends the same request trains people to ignore it.

Behavior drives the branches

Opens decide Email 2's subject strategy. Clicks route to booking recovery. Replies exit the flow entirely.

The automation is there to be invisible — never like a drip campaign.

Objective progression

Reply/book → reply → engagement → final reply/exit.

Micro-conversions are treated as wins — each is a signal to re-route rather than keep pushing.
